Discussion 1: Causes Of Project failures

According to (Lehtinen, et al., 2013) the common cause of project failure is derived from the following categories:

  • 1. People: Factors related to inexperience.
  • 2. Tasks: Factors related to task complexity and output.
  • 3. Methods: Factors related to project processes and monitoring
  • 4. Environment: Factor related to resources, and schedules.

    • Furthermore, I believe that the three most common cause of project failure are the following:


  • Inadequate Planning and Scope Management: Undefined objectives can lead to confusion and constant change in requirements. As a result, an Inaccurate resource estimation may occur which increases the risks of budget overruns and missed deadlines.
  • Ineffective Collaboration and Communication: Communication breakdowns between stakeholders, team members, and management can result in conflicts. The lack of clear communication can affect decision making and will lead to redundancy, reduced productivity and wasted efforts.
  • Poor Risk Management: Failing to assess and identify potential risks at the beginning phases of the project can lead to unexpected delays. This will neglect to develop a mitigation plan that may result in risk escalations and serious issues. Moreover, ignoring external factors such as advancements in technology and changes in market needs can leave the project subject to disruptions.

    • Below are examples of project failures as result of the causes mentioned above:


  • Healthcare.gov (2013). A platform for health insurance plans. The platformed suffered from slow loading times, frequent crashes and incorrect data processing. this came as a result of inadequate planning considering that the project had an ambitious scope within a tight deadline (Anthopoulos, et al., 2016).
  • UK National Programme for IT: An initiative to digitize the healthcare IT system in the UK. Due to the scope expanding over time and the increase of the project complexity created difficulties in managing the requirements leading to budget overrun that eventually resulted in a financial strain (Justinia, 2016).

    Unit 5 Discussion:

    Figure 1 in Minge & Thuring (2018) paper illustrates a CUE model that indicates the emotional reactions based on user perception of a system. In particular, the CUE model seems to highlight the initial user interactions by mainly capturing it’s influence by the usability and visual aesthetics. However, the full adaptation of this model might be redundant considering that this approach might not contribute to long term user satisfaction and loyalty.

    A UX framework that takes into account the evolving nature of user experiences over time might be crucial for creating a user-centric product. According to (Karapanos, et al., 2011), few strategies can be implemented that can incorporate the dynamics of user emotions into a UX framework:

  • User Research:Conducting comprehensive research to understand the target audience, hence mapping the emotional response through different stages of the interaction with the product. This includes finding information on the user’s motivations and expectations when interacting with the system.
  • User Journey:Development of detailed user journey maps that illustrates the emotional states users will encounter during their first use and those that become more experienced.
  • User onboarding:Ensuring that the user’s expectations are managed and that the user is guided through the features of the system at the first glance. In addition, ensuring that the interface is user-friendly to avoid potential frustration and confusion.
  • Personalization:Personalized features tailored based individual preferences ensure controlled emotional response.

    • By integrating these strategies into the UX framework, you can create a more adaptable and user-centered design that accommodates the changing emotional landscape of users from their initial interaction to becoming more experienced with the product.

    Discussion 2: Cryptography

    TrueCrypt is an open-source encryption tool that was available for operating systems such as windows, Mac and Linux. TrueCrypt provided its users with real-time encryption of data by creating a virtual disk and viewing random data until it is decrypted. In 2014, TrueCrypt was discounted and father maintenance operations were ceased (Roach, 2022).

    During the same year, a cryptanalysis conducted on TrueCrypt by Junestam & Guigo (2014) revealed several vulnerabilities, some which are considered high in severity. However, the findings suggest no evidence of intentional malicious code and is rather prone to bugs. From my point of view, I disagree with the authors statement as most of the vulnerabilities that are discovered can be addressed if the code is maintained. On the other hand, the two main potential weaknesses as revealed by the audit presents a huge factor in determining the software’s reliability:

    Volume Header key derivation algorithm: Weak validation of driver letter symbols. TrueCrypt uses the minimum iteration count using the current algorithm in which brute force attack can be performed.

    Sensitive information might be paged out from kernel stacks: A memory overflow can result in stacked data to be paged out to the hard disk. Therefore, allowing the attacker to access non-encrypted confidential data (Baluda, et al., 2015).

    In my opinion, I would not recommend TrueCrypt as an encryption solution to a friend because of the lack of maintainability. All initiatives to fix current vulnerabilities in TrueCrypt are stopped, especially after the publishing of the weakness. Newer encryption tools such as VeraCrypt that provides modern security solutions is still maintained.

    Portfolio Component: Facated Data

    According to Schmitz et al (2016), public and private are two types of faceted data. Failing to ensure secure flow of information may result in a data leakage in both data types. Therefore, faceted values that are assigned to data types protects unauthorised viewers from observing it. Thus, I think it is good practice to protect systems from data leakage, specifically private data. Although this approached contributes to the increased complexity of the code, Schmitz et al (2016) introduces the process of integrating faceted language as a library rather than modifying the actual code.
