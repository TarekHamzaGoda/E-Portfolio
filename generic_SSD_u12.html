Reflections

As continuation of the pervious week’s topic on future trends and the differences between monolithic structure and microservices, according to____ the main differences lie in performance and security. Microkernels are often considered more secure due to their modular and isolated design. A security breach in one component or module is less likely to affect other components or the core kernel. In monolithic structures, a vulnerability in one part of the kernel can potentially compromise the entire system. Monolithic structures can have better performance in certain scenarios, as system functionalities are tightly integrated into a single kernel, reducing the overhead of IPC and context switching. Microkernels, on the other hand, may have slightly higher overhead due to the need for IPC and context switching between components.
